Numidian Product Terms and Risk Inventory Disclaimer

Consumer Dividends Holding Corp

Effective date: June 14, 2026

These terms apply specifically to Numidian. They supplement and, where they conflict, override the master Terms of Service for Numidian use only.

1. What Numidian is

Numidian is a structured risk inventory and scoring tool. You enter the facts of an employment situation across documented input fields. Numidian scores the organizational exposure across five dimensions (retaliation exposure, documentation strength, comparator risk, protected class factor, overall exposure index) using a fixed framework built on EEOC charge data, federal court timing patterns, and a fifteen-state law matrix. The output is a structured report.

Numidian is sold to and used by:

  • Fractional HR consultants advising multiple clients.
  • Employment attorneys evaluating case viability.
  • HR managers at small and mid-sized businesses assessing exposure.
  • Business owners trying to understand their position before retaining counsel.

2. What Numidian is not

Numidian is not legal advice. Using Numidian does not create an attorney-client relationship with Consumer Dividends Holding Corp or anyone associated with it. The structured risk report is a tool to inform a human professional's analysis, not a substitute for it.

Numidian is not an employment decision tool. Numidian does not make, recommend, or materially influence any decision to hire, fire, promote, demote, discipline, train, or otherwise act on any individual employee or applicant. Numidian assesses organizational and litigation exposure for the advisor or manager reading the report. Decisions about employees remain solely with the employer and their counsel.

Numidian is not an automated employment-related decision technology as that term is used in the Connecticut Artificial Intelligence Responsibility and Transparency Act (Public Act 26-15, Section 31-57aa et seq., applying to systems deployed on or after October 1, 2027) for the same reason: the output is not a substantial factor in any employment-related decision about an individual. It is a risk inventory for the firm's internal analysis.

Numidian is not a covered automated decision-making technology as that term is used in the Colorado Automated Decision-Making Technology Act (SB 26-189, effective January 1, 2027) for the same reason. The decision-maker is the employer. Numidian informs the analysis of the employer's advisors. Where a customer chooses to use a Numidian output as an input to a consequential decision about an individual, that customer is the deployer and bears the compliance obligations of the law that applies to them.

3. Customer responsibilities

You agree that:

  • You will use Numidian outputs to inform your own professional analysis, not as a substitute for it.
  • You will not represent Numidian outputs as legal advice to your own clients or to any third party.
  • You will not use Numidian outputs as the basis for an employment decision about any individual without your own independent review and, where required, the advice of licensed counsel.
  • You will input only facts you are authorized to process under your role, your engagement with your client, and applicable law.
  • You will not enter sensitive personal information beyond what is necessary for the risk assessment.

4. Confidentiality of your inputs

Case facts you enter into Numidian are confidential to you. We do not disclose them to anyone except as needed to deliver the report (see our Privacy Policy and Subprocessor List). We do not use your inputs to train or improve any language model. Pro and Agency tier users have access to historical reports through the dashboard. Single-report and 5-pack tier users have access for the duration of their access window.

If you are bound by confidentiality obligations to your own clients, you are responsible for confirming that entering information into a software tool is consistent with those obligations. We provide a Data Processing Addendum to Agency tier customers on request.

5. The scoring framework

Numidian's scoring framework is documented at a high level in our public materials and in detail in customer onboarding for Agency tier. The core elements:

  • Five scoring dimensions (retaliation exposure, documentation strength, comparator risk, protected class factor, overall exposure index).
  • Eighteen documented input fields.
  • A jurisdiction multiplier table covering fifteen states, applied based on the state you enter.
  • Industry and company size adjustments where applicable.

The framework is fixed. The same inputs produce the same scores. We update the framework as case law and EEOC data evolve. Updates are dated and customers are notified of material changes.

8. No defense against discrimination claims

This is worth stating directly. Under the Connecticut Fair Employment Practices Act as amended by the CART Act, the use of an automated employment-related decision technology is not a defense to a discrimination claim. Other states are adopting similar positions. Even though Numidian is not, under the legal definitions referenced in Section 2 above, an AEDT or covered ADMT, our customers should understand: using a software tool to assess your exposure does not insulate you from liability for a discriminatory decision. Numidian helps you see the risk. It does not absolve you of the duty to act lawfully.
