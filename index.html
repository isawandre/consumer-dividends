A dividend for the data economy.

This is not a handout. It is a royalty model for companies that profit from consumer data, attention, behavioral prediction, AI training, and platform activity.

1. Companies monetize consumers

Data brokers, platforms, AI firms, ad networks, and recommendation engines turn human behavior into revenue.

2. A small royalty goes into a fund

A narrow contribution is collected from eligible revenue tied to consumer data and attention.

3. People receive direct payments

The fund is distributed as a periodic Consumer Dividend to eligible adults.

This is less weird than it sounds.

We already use royalty and dividend models when private firms profit from shared resources. The data economy just needs its own version.

FAQ

Fast answers for normal people, skeptics, journalists, and policy goblins.

Is this a tax?

Functionally, yes. Politically and economically, it is better framed as a royalty: companies pay when they monetize consumer data, attention, and behavioral prediction.

Who pays?

Large firms whose revenue depends materially on consumer data, targeted advertising, AI training, recommendation systems, behavioral analytics, or data brokerage.

Who receives the dividend?

Eligible adults. The exact eligibility rules would depend on the final policy design.

Is this UBI?

No. UBI is a general income floor. The Consumer Dividend is tied to monetized consumer value. It is closer to a public royalty than a welfare program.

Would this kill innovation?

A small, simple royalty is cleaner than endless lawsuits, privacy theater, and random fines. If a business model can’t survive paying the people who generate the value, that’s useful information.

How much would people get?

It depends on the revenue base, royalty rate, and eligible population. The calculator gives rough scenarios, not a final promise.
